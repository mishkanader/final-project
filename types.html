Caffeine

Caffeine is considered the world's most widely consumed psychoactive drug. It's a stimulant that affects the Central Nervous System, and is commonly used to prevent drowsiness. The most common forms of ingestion is through beverages and food.

How does it work?

Caffeine mimics Adenosine. Adenosine is a naturally occurring inhibitory neurotransmitter that increases the longer you're awake: hence a decrease in nerve activity and an increase in sleepiness. When Caffeine blocks Adenosine reception, the body doesn't get tired! However, once Caffeine leaves, all the buildup of Adenosine rushes in and you experience a "crash".
